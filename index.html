Sidhaarth Sredharan Murali

Hi! I'm a Master's student in Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) (Fall 2025), focusing on AI Safety & Alignment, World Models, and Reinforcement Learning.

I want to move beyond "natural token processing" (next-token prediction) toward models that learn from delayed rewards and structured tasks. My research centers on building foundations for reasoning in complex multi‑turn tasks, treating research as a sustained effort — something I'm willing to dedicate years to, until I feel I've made real progress toward solving the problem, not just incremental advances.

With Dr. Amrit Singh Bedi at UCF, I developed GRILL, a solver–guider framework for hierarchical credit assignment in LLMs, which showed gains on MBPP and MATH500 benchmarks compared to GRPO. I've explored stabilization strategies for RLHF-style optimization under noisy guider signals, with hands-on experience training/evaluating LLaMA models, building ToM benchmarks, and working on information retrieval for medical QA systems. I've also built red-teaming tools and frameworks for AI safety evaluation.

Looking forward, I want to extend hierarchical RL approaches to settings beyond program synthesis and math reasoning, addressing tasks with deeply delayed feedback. I'm interested in world models beyond language — investigating models that integrate language with other reasoning modalities (spatial, visual, procedural), and studying how agents can learn structured policies that adapt to complex instructions and partially observable environments.

In parallel, I focus on safety and alignment. At Confident AI (YC W25), I built and open‑sourced DeepTeam, a red‑teaming framework for automated adversarial testing of LLMs. I also studied manipulative behavior in language models, resulting in an ACL 2025 main conference paper introducing a new benchmark for manipulation detection. I was recognized with the IndiaAI Fellowship and Travel Grant (2025) and the ACL 2024 Diversity & Inclusion Grant.

During my undergraduate years at NITK, I worked under Dr. Anand Kumar M on backdoor attacks in large language models (LLMs). I also interned in HALE Lab with Dr. Sowmya Kamath on biomedical RAG for question answering.
